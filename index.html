Dr Liang Chen

I am an anthropologist, author, and father of a four-year-old daughter.

My research began with China’s domestic migration during my PhD at the Australian National University, and has since expanded to examine China’s engagements with the Global South.

My teaching and research have taken me across China, Australia, Ethiopia, and Botswana. Through this experience, I bring together local ethnographic insights with broader global processes.

On this website, my work is organised around three interconnected themes. I invite you to explore them through the sections on the right.

Migration, Informal Economy, and Urbanisation in China
PhD Thesis

Getting by in Urban China
This doctoral thesis takes you into a night market on the edge of Nanjing to explore how migrant vendors survive city life. It shows how they rely on relationships—whether with officials, local toughs, or each other—to “get by,” and how this shapes their identities, gender roles, and everyday strategies. It introduces the idea of hun (muddling through) as a key way to understand migrant life in urban China.

Journal Article

Rural Migrants in the Informal Economy of Urbanising China
This article examines the hidden side of China’s rapid urbanisation by focusing on rural migrants working in informal economies. It highlights how their lives are shaped by insecurity and exclusion, while official narratives often gloss over these realities to present development in a positive light.

Chinese and Africans on the Move: Transnationalism, Technology, and Race
Research Article

Strained Technology Transfer in Ethiopia
This study examines Chinese investment in Ethiopia and whether it supports industrial development. It shows that while skills are transferred through workplace training, the process is slow and uneven, shaped by power relations, labour tensions, and corporate priorities rather than ideals of development cooperation.

Policy Article

Chinese Employers in Botswana and Their Impact on Local Workers
This article explores the everyday interactions between Chinese employers and local workers in Botswana. It reveals both opportunities and tensions, showing how business practices, cultural differences, and economic inequalities shape labour relations in a globalising African economy.

Public Essay

Governing Strangers in Guangzhou
Focusing on African migrants in Guangzhou, this article examines how cities manage foreign communities. It explores issues of policing, belonging, and informal economies, offering insight into how migration reshapes urban China and challenges existing systems of governance.

Glocalisation in Africa: University Reform, Cultural Change, and Infrastructure
Book Chapter

University of Botswana Reform
This chapter investigates university strikes in Botswana during COVID‑19. It shows how economic pressures and long-term reforms have reshaped higher education, leading to tensions over pay, governance, and working conditions, and highlighting the effects of neoliberal reforms on African universities.

Journal Article

Indigenising Museum Practices in Botswana
This article examines how museum practices in Botswana can be shaped by indigenous traditions. It shows how local institutions such as age groups and youth troupes play a key role in collecting, performing, and preserving heritage.

Ethnography

Afar Travel Logs
This travel-based ethnographic piece documents how a new road in Ethiopia’s Afar region is transforming local life. It shows how infrastructure reshapes mobility, livelihoods, and belief systems.
